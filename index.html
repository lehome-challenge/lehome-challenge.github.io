Overview

Garment manipulation is a fundamental yet highly challenging problem in robotic manipulation and embodied AI, involving complex, deformable objects and contact-rich interactions. The Garment Manipulation Challenge aims to establish a standardized benchmarking platform for evaluating robotic systems' ability to understand and manipulate garments effectively, fostering innovation in policy learning, tactile reasoning, and visual perception for deformable object handling.

Building on state-of-the-art simulation platforms such as LeRobot and DexGarmentLab, we provide a high-fidelity simulation environment and demonstration datasets to support diverse approaches, including imitation learning, affordance-based methods, and vision-language reasoning. The challenge focuses exclusively on garment folding tasks, primarily on tops (long-sleeve, short-sleeve, sleeveless, with or without hoods), with potential extensions to pants.

The LeHome Challenge 2026 features:

  • Simulation Benchmark with High-Fidelity Technique
  • Real-World Evaluation with Low-Cost Robot Platform
  • Household Scene Tasks Involving Diverse Garments
